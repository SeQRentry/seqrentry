What is SeQRentry?

SeQRentry is a highly secure method to sign in to web sites online by using nothing except your phone and a simple, easy to remember PIN code.

Any web site that uses a username and password can be SeQRentry-enabled within minutes, and the phone app will soon be available for all major mobile operating systems — all totally free of charge.

So try it now, and help making the Internet a more secure place for all of us, no matter if you are an end user, web site master or developer!

Why SeQRentry?

Why should you care? After all, we log in to web sites all the time, and it works just fine, right? No, actually it does not work just fine. Something is rotten in the state of the Internet. Very rotten.

Face reality

It used to be simple. You had one password for email and possibly a second one for MSN or ICQ (remember those?). Perhaps you used the same password for both services. Big deal.

But now? Facebook, Twitter, Google/Gmail, Live/Hotmail, iCloud, iTunes, Amazon, Netflix, Yahoo, eBay, LinkedIn, various forums, web shops and dozens if not hundreds of other sites on the net want you to identify yourself using a name and a password.

Of course you use the same password for most of them. All of us do. How the hell would we rememeber how to log in if not?

Web sites and password databases can be hacked into, many passwords can be brute-forced and the most can simply be guessed with no effort at all.

Worst case scenario

Most web sites can reset your password using a link sent to your email address. If a criminal gains access to your primary or even your secondary email account—you didn't use that one as the recovery address for your primary email account, did you?—he or she can do almost anything: post obscene pictures to Facebook in your name, access your private photo collection, spend your money on Amazon or remote-wipe your iPhone or even computer. Yes, really.

A realistic remedy

Security experts tell you to use long, hard-to-remember passwords and make up a new, unique password for each new service you sign up for. Well, good for them! That's not going to fly.

Some organizations, such as banks, might use custom code boxes, client-side SSL certificates, smart cards or fingerprint sensors. It's not like you always have all that gear with you in your pockets, is it?

So what do you always have with you in you pockets? Yeah, that's right. Your phone.

SeQRentry combines a physical thing you have—your phone—with a PIN-code you know and lets you log in to web sites using those two items combined. That's called Two-factor authentication, and that's probably what your bank uses to protect your savings account. SeQRentry makes this strong authentication method available everywhere.

Benefits for end-users

You can stop worry about somebody breaking in to your user accounts, and you can stop feeling bad for reusing such a pathetic password on more sites that you can remember. You don't have to remember the passwords for each site anymore. In fact, nobody will know the actual password—not you, not we, not the site owner (assuming they hash their users' passwords, and most of them thankfully do) and definately no scumbag criminal.

You don't even have to remember your login name anymore; just scan a QR code with your phone, enter your 4 or 6 digit PIN and SeQRentry with automatically fill in the login name and password for you!

Benefits for web site owners

If your users' passwords are secure, unique and unguessable, nobody is going to mess with your users' accounts. Isn't that fantastic?

SeQRentry works with your existing username/password system. All you need to do is to include a JavaScript file and add a few HTML attributes, and you're done. It's that easy.

For high-volume sites, you probably want to host your own SeQRentry proxy service though. It's a good thing that everything is open source software, then, isn't it?

Benefits for keyring app developers

Developers of mobile keyring apps can even SeQRentry-enable their own apps according to the specifications and let their users send the passwords directly to the web sites using the SeQRentry protocol.

Benefits for criminals

None!
